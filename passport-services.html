Passport Services

Apply for, renew, or replace a Nigerian passport. Biometric enrolment is required for all applications.

  1. Home
  2. Consular Services
  3. Passport Services
Processing Time6–8 Weeks
AppointmentRequired
BiometricsMandatory
Validity5 or 10 Years
⚠️

Nigerian passport applications are processed by the Nigeria Immigration Service. The Mission serves as the official collection and submission point. All applicants must appear in person for biometric data capture. Do not make travel plans until your passport has been collected.

The Mission provides passport services to Nigerian citizens residing in Japan. Applications are submitted through the Mission, processed by the Nigeria Immigration Service headquarters in Abuja, and returned to the Mission for collection. The Mission issues the Nigerian Standard Passport (32 pages, 5-year validity) and the Nigerian Enhanced E-Passport (64 pages, 10-year validity).

New Passport Application

For Nigerian citizens who have never held a passport, or who wish to apply for the new biometric e-Passport.

Requirements

  • Original Nigerian Birth Certificate or Age Declaration Affidavit
  • Certified copy of Nigerian National Identity Card (NIN slip) — NIN is mandatory
  • Two recent passport-size photographs (white background)
  • Completed passport application form (available at the Mission)
  • Evidence of Nigerian citizenship (e.g. previously held passport, citizenship certificate)
  • Any Nigerian visa if in Japan on a foreign travel document
  • Applicable fees (non-refundable)
NGN 25,000 Standard (32 pp, 5 yr)  ·  NGN 35,000 Enhanced (64 pp, 10 yr)

Passport Renewal

For Nigerian citizens whose current passport has expired or is about to expire (within 6 months).

Requirements

  • Current/expired Nigerian passport (original)
  • Nigerian National Identification Number (NIN) — mandatory for all renewals
  • Two recent passport-size photographs
  • Completed passport renewal form
  • Applicable renewal fee
ℹ️

If your previous passport was issued more than 10 years ago, additional proof of citizenship may be required. Contact the Mission for guidance.

Same fees as new passport application.

Lost or Damaged Passport

Report the loss or theft immediately to both local police and to the Mission.

Requirements

  • Official police report confirming the loss or theft (original)
  • Sworn affidavit of loss (can be prepared at the Mission — notarial fee applies)
  • Damaged passport (if applicable)
  • Nigerian National Identification Number (NIN)
  • Two recent passport-size photographs
  • Completed passport application form
  • Any supporting documents proving Nigerian citizenship
  • Applicable fee
⚠️

If you need to travel urgently and cannot wait for a new passport, you may be eligible for an Emergency Travel Certificate.

Child / Minor Passport (Under 18)

Nigerian children are entitled to their own passport. As of 2023, children may no longer be included on a parent's passport.

Requirements

  • Child's Nigerian Birth Certificate (original)
  • Child's NIN (mandatory for all ages)
  • Both parents' valid Nigerian passports or proof of Nigerian citizenship
  • Consent letter from both parents (if one parent is absent, a notarised consent or court order is required)
  • Two recent passport-size photographs of the child
  • Completed application form signed by parent/guardian
  • Applicable fee
ℹ️

Child must be present at the Mission for biometric data capture (photograph and fingerprints where applicable by age).

Follow these 7 steps to apply for or renew your Nigerian passport at this Mission.

Step 01

Obtain Your NIN

A valid NIN is mandatory for all passport applications. Visit NIMC or contact the Mission for assistance obtaining your NIN from abroad.

Applications without a valid NIN will not be processed
Step 02

Book an Appointment

Contact the Passport Section to schedule your appointment. Walk-ins are not accepted. Email passport.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng.

📞 Mon–Fri, 9:00 AM – 12:00 PM
Step 03

Complete the Application Form

Fill out the passport application form carefully. Forms are available at the Mission. Errors may delay processing.

📄 Available at the Mission counter
Step 04

Attend in Person for Biometrics

All applicants — including children of all ages — must appear in person. Biometric data (photograph and fingerprints) will be captured on your appointment day.

Bring all original documents plus certified copies
Step 05

Pay the Application Fee

Pay the applicable fee at the Mission counter. A numbered receipt will be issued. Keep this receipt safe — it is required to collect your passport.

Standard (32 pages, 5 yr)NGN 25,000
Enhanced (64 pages, 10 yr)NGN 35,000
Step 06

Production in Abuja

Your application is transmitted to the Nigeria Immigration Service in Abuja for production. This takes approximately 6–8 weeks.

🏢 Nigeria Immigration Service, Abuja⌛ 6–8 weeks
Step 07

Collect Your Passport

You will be notified when your passport is ready. Collect in person with your receipt and a valid photo ID. An authorised representative may collect with a signed letter.

Passports not collected within 90 days may be returned to Abuja

  • The National Identification Number (NIN) is a unique 11-digit number issued by NIMC to all Nigerian citizens. It is mandatory for passport applications. Visit www.nimc.gov.ng or contact the Mission for guidance on obtaining your NIN from abroad.
