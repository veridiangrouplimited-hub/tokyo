Visa Services

Information for foreign nationals wishing to visit, study, or do business in the Federal Republic of Nigeria.

  1. Home
  2. Consular Services
  3. Visa Services
Processing Time5–10 Working Days
AppointmentRequired
Fee RangeUSD 50–200
ValiditySingle / Multiple Entry

Visa applications must be submitted in person. Do not post applications unless you have received explicit written instructions from the Mission. Fees are non-refundable.

The Mission is authorised to issue Nigerian entry visas to eligible foreign nationals whose countries are not exempt from Nigerian visa requirements. All applications are processed under the Nigerian Immigration Act and the guidelines of the Nigeria Immigration Service.

Citizens of certain countries may be eligible for Visa on Arrival or eVisa through the Nigeria Immigration Service portal. Please confirm your eligibility before visiting the Mission.

For eVisa and Visa on Arrival eligibility, visit the Nigeria Immigration Service website.

Tourist / Visit Visa STR

For tourism, leisure, family visits, or medical treatment in Nigeria. Valid for stays up to 90 days, single or multiple entry.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport — min. 6 months validity, 2 blank pages
  • Completed and signed visa application form
  • 2 passport photographs (white background, 35×45 mm)
  • Proof of sufficient funds (3-month bank statement)
  • Confirmed return flight ticket or travel itinerary
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or host invitation letter)
  • Yellow fever vaccination certificate (mandatory)
USD 50 single entry  ·  USD 100 multiple entry (up to 1 year)

Business Visa STR-B

For business meetings, conferences, trade visits, or commercial activities. Does not authorise employment in Nigeria.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport — min. 6 months validity, 2 blank pages
  • Completed and signed visa application form
  • 2 passport photographs
  • Official invitation letter from a Nigerian company (company letterhead)
  • Business registration of the inviting Nigerian company
  • Return flight ticket or confirmed itinerary
  • Proof of accommodation in Nigeria
  • Applicant's business card or company registration
  • Yellow fever vaccination certificate
USD 100 single entry  ·  USD 200 multiple entry (up to 1 year)

Student Visa STR-S

For foreign nationals admitted to an accredited Nigerian university, polytechnic, or educational institution.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport — min. 6 months validity
  • Completed visa application form
  • 2 passport photographs
  • Original admission/acceptance letter from Nigerian institution
  • Proof of tuition payment or scholarship letter
  • Proof of accommodation in Nigeria
  • Bank statement showing sufficient funds
  • Yellow fever vaccination certificate
USD 75 per year of study

Transit Visa TDV

For passengers transiting through a Nigerian international airport without leaving the transit zone. Valid for 48 hours.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport — min. 6 months validity
  • Confirmed onward flight ticket and itinerary
  • Valid visa for destination country (where required)
  • Completed transit visa application form
USD 25 (48-hour transit)

Official / Diplomatic Visa

For foreign government officials, diplomats, and members of international organisations travelling to Nigeria on official duty. Must be submitted through the applicant's foreign ministry.

Required Documents

  • Valid diplomatic or official passport
  • Diplomatic note from the applicant's foreign ministry
  • Official travel orders or invitation from a Nigerian government institution
  • Completed application form
Reciprocal basis  ·  Exempt for diplomatic passport holders (conditions apply)

Follow these steps to submit your Nigerian visa application at the Mission in Tokyo.

  1. 1

    Book an Appointment

    Contact the Consular Section by email or phone to secure an appointment slot. Walk-in visits are not accepted for visa applications.

    Book via Contact Page →
  2. 2

    Complete the Application Form

    Download and fully complete the Nigerian Visa Application Form. All fields must be filled accurately — incomplete or inconsistent forms will be rejected.

    Download Form (PDF) →
  3. 3

    Gather Your Documents

    Collect all supporting documents required for your visa category. Bring original documents plus two clear photocopies of each. See the for the full checklist.

  4. 4

    Submit in Person & Pay the Fee

    Attend your appointment at the Mission. Submit your completed form and documents at the Consular counter. Biometric data may be collected. Pay the applicable visa fee at this point — fees are non-refundable.

  5. 5

    Await Processing

    Standard processing takes 5–10 working days. The Mission recommends applying at least 4 weeks before travel. You will be notified when your visa is ready — or if the application is refused.

  6. 6

    Collect Your Passport

    Collect your passport in person or through an authorised representative. Bring your collection receipt and valid ID. Representatives must provide a signed authorisation letter.
