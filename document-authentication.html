Document Authentication

Authenticate Nigerian-issued documents for legal use in Japan and other jurisdictions.

Processing Time3–5 Working Days
AppointmentRequired

Document authentication (also known as legalisation or apostille) is the process by which the Mission confirms the authenticity of the signature, seal, or stamp of a Nigerian public official on a document, so that it may be accepted as valid in Japan or in international transactions.

The Mission authenticates Nigerian government-issued documents already verified by the relevant issuing authority in Nigeria. The Mission does not verify the content of documents — it confirms the authenticity of official signatures and seals.

⚠️

Important: Documents must first be authenticated by the relevant Nigerian authority (e.g. Federal Ministry of Education, NYSC, CAC) before submission to the Mission. Documents not processed through the appropriate Nigerian authority will be returned.

Types of Documents Authenticated

📚 Educational

  • University degree certificates
  • Diplomas and transcripts
  • WAEC/NECO certificates
  • NYSC discharge/exemption certificate
  • Professional qualifications

👨‍👩‍👧 Personal / Civil

  • Birth & marriage certificates
  • Divorce decrees
  • Death certificates
  • Adoption orders
  • Change of name documents

⚖️ Legal

  • Court orders and judgments
  • Power of attorney
  • Affidavits & sworn statements
  • Police clearance certificates
  • Notarised declarations

🏢 Corporate

  • Certificate of incorporation (CAC)
  • Memorandum & articles of association
  • Board resolutions
  • Annual returns & trade licences
  • Tax clearance certificates

Documents Required

  • Original document(s) for authentication
  • Clear photocopy of each document (two copies)
  • Evidence of prior authentication by the relevant Nigerian authority (where applicable)
  • Applicant's valid Nigerian passport or other government-issued photo ID
  • Completed authentication request form (available at the Mission)
  • Applicable fee per document

Authentication Process

  1. 1

    Authenticate in Nigeria First

    Ensure your document has been authenticated by the relevant issuing ministry or authority in Nigeria (e.g. Federal Ministry of Education for academic certificates). This is the mandatory first step.

  2. 2

    Obtain Apostille (if applicable)

    Nigeria acceded to the Hague Apostille Convention. For documents destined for Hague Convention countries, an Apostille may be required. Confirm with the receiving party.

  3. 3

    Book an Appointment

    Contact the Mission to schedule an appointment. Bring all original documents and two copies each.

    Contact the Mission →
  4. 4

    Submit at the Mission

    Submit documents at the consular counter. A consular officer will review them and advise if any additional steps are required.

  5. 5

    Pay the Authentication Fee

    Pay the fee per document. A receipt will be issued. Processing takes 3–5 working days.

  6. 6

    Collect Authenticated Documents

    Collect in person or through an authorised representative with your receipt.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Authentication is the process by which a government official verifies the signature of another official. An Apostille is a specific form of authentication between countries that have signed the Hague Convention of 1961. Nigeria acceded to this Convention. For Hague Convention countries, an Apostille from the appropriate Nigerian authority is often sufficient. For non-Hague countries, consular legalisation through the Mission may also be required. Confirm the specific requirements with the receiving authority in Japan.

  • Postal submission is generally not accepted due to the risk of loss of original documents. Contact the Mission to discuss your circumstances. If you are unable to attend in person, a properly authorised representative may submit on your behalf.
