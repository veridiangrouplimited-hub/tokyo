Mission Overview

The Embassy of the Federal Republic of Nigeria in Japan is the official diplomatic representation of Nigeria, established pursuant to the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations. The Mission is responsible for protecting and promoting the interests of Nigeria and Nigerian citizens in Japan, while fostering cordial bilateral relations between the two nations.

The Mission operates under the authority of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Federal Republic of Nigeria, Abuja. It is headed by an Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary, appointed by and answerable to the President of the Federal Republic of Nigeria.

ℹ️

Office Hours: The Mission is open to the public Monday to Friday, 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM, excluding Nigerian and Japan public holidays. Consular services operate from 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM (by appointment only).

Diplomatic Mandate

The Mission operates in accordance with the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations (1961) and the Foreign Policy objectives of the Federal Republic of Nigeria. Its core mandate includes:

  • Representing the Federal Republic of Nigeria in Japan and protecting Nigerian national interests
  • Providing consular services to Nigerian citizens and eligible foreign nationals
  • Promoting bilateral political, economic, and cultural relations between Nigeria and Japan
  • Facilitating trade, investment, and business opportunities for Nigerian and host-country enterprises
  • Supporting the Nigerian diaspora community with welfare, documentation, and emergency assistance
  • Reporting on political, economic, and social developments in Japan to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs
  • Participating in multilateral diplomacy and supporting Nigeria's engagement with international organisations
  • Promoting Nigerian culture, heritage, and values in Japan
