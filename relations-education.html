Educational Cooperation

Academic partnerships, student exchange programmes, scholarships, and research collaboration between Nigeria and Japan.

  1. Home
  2. Nigeria–Japan
  3. Educational Cooperation

Building Knowledge Bridges

Education and human capital development are central pillars of the Nigeria–Japan bilateral relationship. Through a Memorandum of Understanding on Educational Cooperation, both countries have established pathways for student exchange, academic collaboration, and capacity building that serve the long-term interests of both nations.

Areas of Cooperation

01

Student Exchange Programmes

Bilateral student exchange programmes enable Nigerian students to study in Japan universities and vice versa, fostering cross-cultural academic understanding.

02

Government Scholarships

Both governments offer scholarships for postgraduate study. Nigerian students interested in government-sponsored programmes should contact the Mission for current opportunities and application deadlines.

03

University Twinning

Nigerian and Japan universities have established twinning agreements enabling joint degrees, faculty exchanges, and shared research infrastructure.

04

Technical & Vocational Training

Cooperation in TVET (Technical and Vocational Education and Training) to transfer skills in engineering, healthcare, ICT, and agri-technology to Nigeria.

05

Research & Innovation

Joint research grants and innovation hubs support collaborative scientific work in areas of mutual priority including health, climate, and sustainable development.

Nigerian Students in Japan — Advisory

  • Register with the Nigerian Embassy upon arrival (see Registration)
  • Ensure your student visa and residence permit are always valid
  • Join the Nigerian Students' Association in Japan for peer support
  • Contact the Mission's Consular Section for advice on documentation, legal issues, or welfare concerns
  • Keep your Nigerian passport up to date — apply for renewal at least 6 months before expiry
For scholarship enquiries and student welfare matters, contact the Cultural & Public Diplomacy Section: culture.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng
