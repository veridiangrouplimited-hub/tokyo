Notarial Services

Witness signatures, certify documents, and administer oaths for Nigerians residing in Japan.

  1. Home
  2. Consular Services
  3. Notarial Services
Processing TimeSame Day
AppointmentRequired
In-PersonMandatory
EligibilityNigerian Citizens

The Mission provides a range of notarial services to Nigerian citizens residing in Japan. Consular officers are authorised under the Consular Conventions Act to perform notarial acts on behalf of Nigerian citizens abroad. These services are for documents intended for use within Nigeria.

All notarial acts must be performed in person at the Mission. The applicant must appear before the consular officer, provide valid identification, and sign or declare in the officer's presence where required.

ℹ️

For notarisation of documents for use in Japan, you will need to consult a locally licenced notary public — the Mission cannot notarise documents for use in Japan.

Services Offered

Witnessing Signatures

Witness your signature on statutory declarations, consent forms, deeds, and powers of attorney.

Certified True Copies

Certification that a photocopy is a true and accurate copy of the original document presented.

Oaths & Affirmations

Administration of oaths or affirmations for affidavits, statutory declarations, and sworn statements for Nigerian proceedings.

Affidavits

Preparation and witnessing of affidavits of loss, affidavits of identity, and other sworn statements.

Consent Letters

Witnessing of parental consent letters, travel consent for minors, and other consent documents.

Power of Attorney

Witnessing of power of attorney documents authorising someone to act on your behalf in Nigeria.

Documents Required

  • Valid Nigerian passport or government-issued photo identification
  • The original document(s) requiring the notarial service
  • Any supporting documents relevant to the notarial act (e.g. supporting evidence for an affidavit)
  • Applicable fee (payable at the Mission)
  • Physical presence at the Mission — all notarial acts must be performed in person

  • For witnessing signatures and certified true copies, you should bring the prepared document. For affidavits and statutory declarations, a consular officer can assist with preparation at the Mission (a higher fee applies). Contact the Mission in advance to confirm what is needed.

  • Notarial services are primarily for Nigerian citizens. In exceptional circumstances where the document relates directly to Nigerian legal proceedings or Nigerian parties, the Mission may consider requests from non-Nigerians. Contact the Consular Section in advance to discuss your situation.

  • The Mission can certify multiple copies. A fee applies per page. Bring the original and the number of copies you require — the consular officer will compare each copy against the original before certifying.
