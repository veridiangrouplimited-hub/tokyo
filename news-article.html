Nigeria and Japan Sign New Landmark Bilateral Trade Agreement

3 June 2026 ✍️ Embassy of Nigeria, Tokyo 📂 Press Releases 🕐 5 min read
⬇️ Download PDF
🤝

Tokyo, Japan — 3 June 2026. The Embassy of the Federal Republic of Nigeria and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Japan today signed a landmark Bilateral Trade and Investment Agreement, marking a significant milestone in the long-standing diplomatic and economic relationship between the two countries.

The signing ceremony was attended by H.E. [Ambassador's Name], Ambassador Extraordinary and Plenipotentiary of Nigeria to Japan, and H.E. [Host Country Minister's Name], Minister of Foreign Affairs of Japan. Senior officials from both governments, business leaders, and members of the diplomatic corps were also in attendance.

Key Provisions of the Agreement

The agreement establishes a comprehensive framework for expanded bilateral trade and investment, with both governments committing to the following objectives:

  • Increase bilateral trade from the current $[X] billion to $2 billion annually within five years
  • Remove tariff and non-tariff barriers on priority goods and services
  • Establish a Joint Trade and Investment Committee to meet annually
  • Facilitate greater private sector engagement and business-to-business partnerships
  • Cooperate on investment promotion, regulatory harmonisation, and market access
  • Support small and medium enterprises in both countries to access bilateral markets

Statement from the Ambassador

"Today's agreement reflects the strength and depth of our bilateral relationship. Nigeria and Japan are natural partners — we share values, complementary economies, and a mutual desire to create prosperity for our citizens. This agreement is a concrete commitment to turning that potential into reality. I look forward to seeing Nigerian and Japan businesses benefit directly from this new framework."
— H.E. [Ambassador's Name], Ambassador of Nigeria to Japan

Background

Nigeria and Japan have maintained diplomatic relations since [Year]. Bilateral trade has grown steadily over recent years, with Japan now ranking among Nigeria's top [X] trading partners in [Region]. The two countries share active cooperation in areas including agriculture, energy, education, and technology.

This agreement builds on the foundation of previous bilateral instruments, including the Double Taxation Agreement ([Year]) and the Cultural Cooperation MOU ([Year]), and is expected to unlock significant new business opportunities for enterprises in both countries.

Next Steps

Following today's signing, both governments will undertake the necessary domestic processes for ratification and entry into force of the agreement. The inaugural meeting of the Joint Trade and Investment Committee is expected to take place within 90 days.

Businesses and investors interested in opportunities arising from the agreement are invited to contact the Mission's Trade and Investment section at trade.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng.
