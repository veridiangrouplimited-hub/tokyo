How the Mission Can Help

The Mission has a duty of care to all Nigerian citizens in Japan. Consular officers will do their best to assist within the limits of their legal authority. Select a situation below to see how we can support you.

Arrest & Detention Support

Under the Vienna Convention on Consular Relations, you have the right to have Nigeria's Consulate informed of your arrest. Here is exactly what happens:

1

Assert Your Right

Tell the police: "I am a Nigerian citizen and I request that the Nigerian Embassy be informed."

Mission Notified

Local authorities must notify the Mission without delay. We will contact you and your family.

3

Consular Visit

A consular officer visits you in custody, confirms your welfare, and provides a list of local lawyers.

4

Ongoing Support

We monitor your case, stay in contact with family, and advocate for fair treatment under local law.

What we cannot do

  • Interfere in local legal or judicial processes
  • Pay bail, legal fees, or fines on your behalf
  • Act as your legal representative or lawyer
  • Force the government to release you

Medical Emergency Support

If you are hospitalised or seriously ill in Japan, our consular team can provide the following support:

1

Contact the Mission

Call our emergency line or ask a friend / hospital staff to contact us on your behalf immediately.

2

Welfare Visit

A consular officer will visit you in hospital and verify your situation and welfare.

3

Family Notification

With your consent, we will notify your family in Nigeria and keep them updated on your condition.

4

Medical Liaison & Evacuation

We liaise with medical staff and, where clinically necessary, advise on medical evacuation options.

What we cannot do

  • Pay hospital fees or medical bills
  • Make medical decisions on your behalf
  • Guarantee medical evacuation arrangements

Lost or Stolen Travel Documents

If your passport or travel documents are lost or stolen, act quickly. We can issue an Emergency Travel Certificate to get you home.

1

Report to Police

File a police report immediately. You will need this document for the Emergency Travel Certificate application.

2

Contact the Mission

Call or email the Mission. Explain your situation and confirm you have filed a police report.

3

Book Appointment

Schedule an urgent appointment at the Mission. Bring the police report and any proof of identity you have.

4

Receive ETC

An Emergency Travel Certificate is issued, allowing you to travel back to Nigeria immediately.

Learn About Emergency Travel Certificates →

Death of a Nigerian in Japan

The Mission extends its support to the families of Nigerian citizens who have passed away in Japan. We assist with the following:

Death Registration
Guidance on obtaining a local death certificate recognised by Nigerian authorities.
Family Notification
Official notification of the death to next of kin in Nigeria through proper diplomatic channels.
Repatriation Advice
Advice on the process and requirements for repatriation of remains to Nigeria.
Funeral Referrals
A list of reputable funeral homes and embalming services available in Japan.
Correspondence Support
Assistance with communications between next of kin and local authorities or institutions.
NIN/Documentation
Guidance on Nigerian death registration where required for estate and legal purposes.
⚠️

The Mission does not bear any costs associated with the repatriation of remains. Costs are the responsibility of the next of kin. Travel insurance is strongly recommended for all Nigerians living or travelling abroad.

Victim of Crime Support

If you have been the victim of a crime in Japan, the Mission can provide the following guidance and support:

1

Ensure Your Safety

Get to a safe location. Call local emergency services (police, ambulance) if needed.

2

Contact the Mission

Call our emergency line or email the Mission. We will advise on next steps and provide referrals.

3

File a Police Report

A consular officer can assist you with the police reporting process and act as a point of contact.

4

Referral & Follow-up

We refer you to local victim support organisations and continue to monitor your welfare.

🛡️

Trafficking or Forced Labour? Contact our emergency line immediately. We work with local authorities and welfare organisations to ensure your safety and, where appropriate, assist with your return to Nigeria.

Emergency Repatriation

In extreme cases of vulnerability or distress — including destitution, trafficking, or severe welfare emergencies — the Mission may assist with repatriation arrangements. Eligibility is assessed case by case.

1

Contact the Mission

Call the emergency line. Explain your situation — any repatriation request is treated with the utmost priority.

2

Case Assessment

A consular officer evaluates your situation and determines eligibility under government repatriation policy.

3

Travel Documentation

The Mission facilitates an Emergency Travel Certificate if required and coordinates with Nigerian authorities.

4

Repatriation Arranged

Where approved, travel arrangements are coordinated and the relevant Nigerian agency is notified for reception.

Eligibility Note

  • Repatriation is not guaranteed and is assessed on a case-by-case basis
  • Applicants must demonstrate genuine vulnerability or distress
  • The Mission acts within government policy and available resources

What the Mission Cannot Do

It is important to understand the limits of consular assistance. These boundaries exist under international law and government policy:

✗Interfere in the legal or judicial processes of Japan
✗Pay your legal fees, bail, fines, or personal debts
✗Provide legal representation or act as your lawyer
✗Force a foreign government to release you from custody
✗Provide financial assistance or loans (except in exceptional welfare cases)
✗Obtain preferential treatment for you under local law
✗Investigate crimes or act as a police authority
✗Pay hospital bills, medical fees, or repatriation costs
