For Nigerian citizens who have lost their passport or travel documents and need to travel urgently.

Genuine Emergency Only: The Emergency Travel Certificate (ETC) is issued solely to Nigerian citizens who have lost or had their passport stolen and require urgent travel to Nigeria. It is not a substitute for a standard passport and will be accepted only for direct travel to Nigeria.

What is an Emergency Travel Certificate?

An Emergency Travel Certificate (ETC) is a temporary, single-journey travel document issued by the Mission to Nigerian citizens stranded in Japan due to the loss, theft, or destruction of their Nigerian passport. The ETC permits travel directly to Nigeria only — it is not valid for travel to any other country.

Upon returning to Nigeria, you must immediately apply for a new passport. The ETC is surrendered at your Nigerian port of entry on arrival.

Who is Eligible?

The ETC is available to Nigerian citizens who:

  • Are a confirmed Nigerian citizen, verifiable through records or supporting documents
  • Have lost, had stolen, or had their passport damaged beyond use in Japan
  • Have an urgent and genuine need to travel to Nigeria (e.g. family emergency, deportation, medical reason, expiry of visa/permit)
  • Cannot wait for a full passport to be processed (6–8 weeks)

Documents Required

  • Official police report confirming the loss or theft (must be filed before visiting the Mission)
  • Any available evidence of Nigerian identity (old passport copy, Nigerian ID, birth certificate, driver's licence)
  • Nigerian National Identification Number (NIN) — if available
  • Two recent passport-size photographs (white background)
  • Signed personal statement explaining the circumstances of the loss
  • Proof of intended travel to Nigeria (confirmed flight booking or ticket)
  • Evidence of lawful residence in Japan (residence permit, visa, or similar)
  • Applicable fee
ℹ️

If you do not have any identification documents, a consular officer will interview you to verify identity. The Mission may contact family members in Nigeria to assist with verification.

How to Apply

  1. 1

    Contact the Mission Immediately

    Call the emergency line or visit the Mission during office hours. For after-hours emergencies, use the dedicated emergency number.

  2. 2

    File a Police Report

    Report the loss or theft to the local police and obtain a written report. This is mandatory for all ETC applications.

  3. 3

    Attend the Mission

    Appear in person with all available documents. A consular officer will interview you to verify your identity and assess eligibility.

  4. 4

    Application Processed

    If approved, the ETC is typically issued within hours. Complex cases may require up to 48 hours for verification.

  5. 5

    Travel to Nigeria

    Use the ETC to travel directly to Nigeria. Surrender it at your Nigerian port of entry. Apply for a new passport immediately upon your return.
