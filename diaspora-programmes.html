Diaspora Programmes

Government initiatives connecting Nigerians abroad with opportunities at home and supporting their welfare overseas.

For Nigerians in Japan

Engaging the Diaspora

The Federal Government of Nigeria recognises the vital role of Nigerians abroad — through remittances exceeding USD 20 billion annually, and the transfer of skills and networks that accelerate Nigeria's development. The following programmes are available to Nigerians in Japan.

Nigerians in Japan Organisation (NIDO)

NIDO Japan is the officially recognised association of Nigerians living and working in Japan. Established under the auspices of the Federal Government, NIDO serves as a bridge between the diaspora and Nigeria's development institutions.

The Embassy works closely with NIDO on welfare, investment, and advocacy matters. Membership is open to all Nigerians resident in Japan.

Contact the Mission to connect with the NIDO Japan Chapter: community.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng

What NIDO Offers

  • Regular community engagement events and town halls with the Ambassador
  • Formal liaison with Nigerian government agencies and ministries
  • Skills and investment matchmaking with Nigerian counterparts
  • Youth mentorship and leadership development programmes
  • Collective advocacy on diaspora policy with the Federal Government

Diaspora Investment Programme

The Nigerian Investment Promotion Commission (NIPC) maintains a dedicated diaspora investment portal to connect Nigerians abroad with investment opportunities across agriculture, manufacturing, healthcare, real estate, and technology.

The Embassy can facilitate introductions to NIPC and other relevant agencies. Nigeria's economy offers compelling opportunities for Japanese-based diaspora investors.

Investment enquiries: economic.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng

Programme Features

  • Diaspora investment visa facilitation (NIPC)
  • Land and real estate investment advisory
  • Connection to the Presidential Enabling Business Environment Council (PEBEC)
  • Access to special economic zones and export processing zones
  • Introduction to Nigeria's priority sectors under the Renewed Hope Agenda

Skills Transfer Programme (STP)

The STP enables highly qualified Nigerians in Japan — scientists, engineers, academics, medical professionals, and technologists — to contribute their expertise to Nigeria's development through structured short-term engagements.

Participants may engage through short-term placements, virtual mentorship, research partnerships, or advisory roles with Nigerian institutions and government agencies.

STP enquiries: culture.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng

Engagement Options

  • Short-term technical assignments in Nigeria (2–6 weeks)
  • Virtual teaching and mentoring roles at Nigerian universities
  • Research partnerships with academic institutions in Nigeria
  • Advisory roles with government ministries and agencies
  • Nigeria–Japan science and technology exchange

Remittance Facilitation

The Embassy works with the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) to promote safe, regulated, and cost-effective remittance channels. Nigerians in Japan send significant sums home each year — using the right channels maximises every yen.

Nigerians in Japan are advised to use CBN-licensed money transfer operators and to take advantage of the CBN's Naira4Dollar scheme which offers competitive rates for diaspora remittances.

Remittance enquiries: consular.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng

Key Information

  • Always use CBN-licenced operators for remittances to Nigeria
  • CBN Naira4Dollar scheme: competitive rates for diaspora transfers
  • List of licensed remittance operators in Japan (available from the Mission)
  • Guidance on avoiding unlicensed and fraudulent operators
  • Report suspicious remittance operators to the Embassy
