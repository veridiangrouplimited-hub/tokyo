How We Work

Mission Departments

The Embassy is structured into specialist sections, each responsible for a distinct area of diplomatic and consular activity.

The Political Section manages the day-to-day diplomatic relationship between Nigeria and Japan. It is responsible for political reporting to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Abuja, coordinating high-level visits and official engagements, and representing Nigeria's positions at multilateral fora in Japan.

The section works closely with Japan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA), the diplomatic corps in Tokyo, and international organisations including the UN-affiliated bodies headquartered in Japan.

Key Functions

  • High-level diplomatic exchanges and protocol management
  • Political analysis and reporting to Abuja
  • Coordination with Japan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs
  • Engagement with international organisations in Japan
  • Multilateral diplomacy and treaty affairs
political.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng

The Consular Section is the primary point of contact for Nigerian citizens in Japan and for foreign nationals seeking Nigerian visas. It is the most public-facing department of the Mission, processing thousands of applications annually.

Opening hours: Monday–Friday, 9:00 AM – 4:00 PM. Passport and visa submissions are by appointment. Walk-in services are available for emergencies and urgent consular matters.

Key Services

  • Nigerian passport issuance and renewal
  • Visa services for foreign nationals
  • Document authentication and legalisation
  • Emergency travel certificates
  • Notarial services and affidavits
  • Consular assistance to Nigerians in distress
consular.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng

The Economic Section promotes bilateral trade and investment between Nigeria and Japan. It works closely with the Nigerian Export Promotion Council (NEPC), the Nigerian Investment Promotion Commission (NIPC), and Japan business associations to facilitate commercial partnerships and attract Japanese investment to Nigeria.

The section also monitors economic developments in Japan relevant to Nigeria and provides economic reporting to Abuja's Ministry of Finance and Central Bank of Nigeria.

Key Functions

  • Business matchmaking and trade facilitation
  • Investment promotion in Nigeria's priority sectors
  • Economic research and market intelligence
  • Organisation of trade fairs and business forums
  • Engagement with JETRO and Japan Chamber of Commerce
economic.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng

The Cultural Section promotes Nigeria's rich cultural heritage and manages public diplomacy activities. It organises cultural events, engages Nigerian community organisations, and serves as the Mission's interface with media and the public in Japan.

The section is also responsible for supporting Nigerian students and academics in Japan, engaging the Nigerian diaspora community, and coordinating national day celebrations and official receptions.

Key Functions

  • Nigerian cultural festivals and heritage exhibitions
  • Engagement with Nigerian community associations
  • Media relations and press releases
  • Public information and outreach
  • Support for Nigerian students in Japan
  • National Day ceremonies and diplomatic receptions
culture.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng

The Administration Section supports all operational functions of the Mission. It manages financial reporting to Nigeria's Ministry of Finance, human resources for locally-engaged staff, facilities management, and procurement in compliance with Nigerian Government financial regulations.

The section ensures the Mission's day-to-day operational continuity, staff welfare, and adherence to Nigeria's public financial management standards.

Key Functions

  • Mission financial management and reporting
  • Human resources and staff welfare
  • Facilities and property management
  • Procurement and logistics
  • IT infrastructure and communications
admin.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng
