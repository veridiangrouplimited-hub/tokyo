Mandate & Functions

The diplomatic responsibilities of the Nigerian Mission under international law and Nigeria's foreign policy.

Our Diplomatic Mandate

The Nigerian Mission in Japan operates under the authority of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Federal Republic of Nigeria. Our mandate is governed by the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations (1961) and the Vienna Convention on Consular Relations (1963), as well as the bilateral agreements in force between Nigeria and Japan.

Representation

Representing the Federal Republic of Nigeria and its Government in all dealings with the Government of Japan and its institutions.

Protection

Protecting the interests of Nigeria and its nationals in Japan within the limits permitted by international law.

Negotiation

Negotiating agreements and treaties with the Government of Japan on behalf of the Federal Government of Nigeria.

Information & Reporting

Ascertaining by all lawful means conditions and developments in Japan, and reporting to the Government of Nigeria.

Consular Services

Issuing travel documents, visas, and providing assistance to Nigerians abroad in accordance with the Vienna Convention on Consular Relations.

Promoting Relations

Promoting friendly relations and developing economic, cultural, and scientific relations between Nigeria and Japan.

Nigeria's Foreign Policy Pillars

The Mission's activities are guided by the five pillars of Nigeria's foreign policy as articulated by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs:

01

Africa as Centrepiece

Nigeria's foreign policy is fundamentally Africa-centred. The Mission actively supports African Union positions and promotes pan-African solidarity.

02

Economic Diplomacy

Leveraging diplomatic ties to attract foreign direct investment, promote Nigerian exports, and position Nigeria as a destination for trade and business.

03

Citizen Diplomacy

Placing Nigerian citizens at the heart of foreign policy — ensuring their welfare, rights, and opportunities abroad are protected and promoted.

04

Peace and Security

Contributing to international peace, security, and conflict resolution through active multilateral engagement and support for UN and AU peacekeeping.

05

Global Governance

Advocating for reform of international institutions to ensure fair representation of Africa and the Global South, including a permanent seat on the UN Security Council.

Legal Basis: The functions of this Mission are governed by the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations (1961), the Vienna Convention on Consular Relations (1963), the Nigerian Constitution (1999 as amended), and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs Act.
