Cultural Relations

The cultural relationship between Nigeria and Japan is a vibrant and growing one. Nigerian culture — encompassing music, film (Nollywood), literature, fashion, cuisine, and the arts — has made a significant impression on the cultural landscape of Japan, just as Japan's culture has enriched Nigeria's creative industries.

The Mission actively promotes Nigerian culture through events, exhibitions, film screenings, and cultural festivals. Cultural exchange programmes facilitate the movement of artists, musicians, and cultural practitioners between the two countries.

🎬

Nollywood & Film

Nigerian cinema is among the most prolific in the world. The Mission promotes Nollywood in Japan through screenings and partnerships with local film festivals.

🎵

Music & Arts

Afrobeats and other Nigerian musical genres have achieved global popularity. The Mission supports Nigerian artists and cultural events in Japan.

📚

Literature

Nigeria has produced some of Africa's most celebrated writers. The Mission promotes Nigerian literature through book fairs, readings, and school outreach programmes.

🎊

Cultural Festivals

The Mission organises and participates in cultural festivals celebrating Nigerian heritage, traditions, and contemporary culture.
