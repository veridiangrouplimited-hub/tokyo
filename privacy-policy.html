Privacy Policy

How the Nigerian Mission collects, uses, and protects your personal information.

  1. Home
  2. Privacy Policy
Last Updated: 1 June 2026Version: 1.0Applies to: nigerianembassy.[xx]

The Embassy of the Federal Republic of Nigeria in Japan ("the Mission", "we", "us") is committed to protecting the privacy and personal information of all individuals who interact with our website and services. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal information in accordance with applicable data protection laws and the guidelines of the Federal Republic of Nigeria.

By using this website or submitting information to the Mission, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy.

1. Information We Collect

We collect personal information only when it is necessary for the provision of consular services, website functionality, or to respond to your enquiries. The types of information we may collect include:

Information You Provide Directly

  • Contact details (name, email address, telephone number, postal address)
  • Identification information (nationality, date of birth, passport number — for consular service applications only)
  • Application data submitted for visa, passport, document authentication, or other consular services
  • Diaspora registration details
  • Enquiry or message content submitted through the contact form

Information Collected Automatically

  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • IP address (anonymised where possible)
  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • Referring website
  • Date and time of visit

We do not collect unnecessary personal information. We request only what is required to provide the relevant service or respond to your enquiry.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use personal information for the following purposes:

  • To process and respond to consular service applications (visa, passport, document authentication, etc.)
  • To respond to general enquiries submitted through the contact form or by email
  • To maintain the diaspora registration database and send relevant alerts and advisories
  • To send official notices, holiday alerts, and service updates to registered users who have consented to receive them
  • To improve the functionality and content of the website
  • To comply with legal obligations and government reporting requirements
  • To protect the safety and welfare of Nigerian citizens in Japan

We will not use your personal information for marketing purposes, sell it to third parties, or share it with any commercial organisation without your explicit consent.

3. Legal Basis for Processing

The Mission processes personal information on the following legal bases:

  • Consent: Where you have given your explicit consent (e.g. diaspora registration, newsletter subscriptions)
  • Contractual necessity: Where processing is necessary to provide a service you have requested (e.g. consular applications)
  • Legal obligation: Where processing is required by law or government directive
  • Legitimate interests: Where processing is necessary to operate the website securely and effectively

4. Cookies

This website uses cookies to improve your browsing experience and to analyse website traffic. Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser. We use the following types of cookies:

  • Essential cookies: Required for the website to function correctly. Cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: Used to understand how visitors use the website (e.g. page views, session duration). These are anonymised and do not identify you personally.
  • Preference cookies: Used to remember your language preference and other settings.

You can control and disable non-essential cookies through the cookie notice displayed when you first visit the website, or through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect the functionality of some parts of the website.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your personal information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Nigeria: Consular application data is transmitted securely to relevant Nigerian government agencies (e.g. Nigeria Immigration Service, NIMC) for processing purposes.
  • Legal requirement: Where required by the law of Nigeria or Japan, or by order of a court or government authority.
  • Safety: Where disclosure is necessary to protect the life, safety, or welfare of a Nigerian citizen.
  • Service providers: We may use trusted third-party service providers (e.g. web hosting, email services) who are contractually bound to protect your data and use it only for the purposes we specify.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third party for commercial purposes.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as it is necessary for the purposes for which it was collected:

  • Consular application records: Retained for a minimum of 7 years in accordance with government record-keeping requirements.
  • Diaspora registration data: Retained until the individual withdraws consent or requests deletion.
  • Contact form enquiries: Retained for 12 months after the matter is resolved, then securely deleted.
  • Website analytics data: Retained for a maximum of 26 months in anonymised form.

7. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the following rights in relation to your personal information:

  • Access: The right to request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction: The right to request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Deletion: The right to request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal retention obligations.
  • Withdrawal of consent: The right to withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent.
  • Objection: The right to object to processing of your personal information in certain circumstances.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at info.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information from unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures include:

  • HTTPS/SSL encryption for all website traffic
  • Secure server hosting with access controls
  • Role-based access control for staff
  • Regular security reviews and updates
  • Staff training on data protection and information security

No method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. We encourage you not to share sensitive personal information (such as full passport numbers or financial details) through the website contact form — use direct, secure email instead.

9. Third-Party Links

This website contains links to external websites, including other Nigerian government portals and relevant Japan resources. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of those websites. We encourage you to read the privacy policies of any external site you visit.

10. Children's Privacy

This website is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children without parental or guardian consent. Where consular services involve minors, the relevant parent or guardian must provide consent and be present at the Mission.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. The updated policy will be published on this page with a revised "Last Updated" date. We encourage you to review this page periodically.

12. Contact Us

If you have any questions, concerns, or complaints about this Privacy Policy or our handling of your personal information, please contact us:

  • Post: Data Protection Officer, Embassy of Nigeria, [Address], Tokyo, Japan
