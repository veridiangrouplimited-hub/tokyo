Cultural Relations

Celebrating the richness of Nigerian culture and the bonds of people-to-people friendship with Japan.

  1. Home
  2. Nigeria–Japan
  3. Cultural Relations

Culture as a Bridge

Nigeria's cultural richness — spanning over 250 ethnic groups, languages, traditions, music, film, literature, and cuisine — is one of its most powerful diplomatic assets. The Nigerian Mission in Japan actively promotes cultural diplomacy as a means of deepening mutual understanding and strengthening people-to-people ties.

Nigerian culture has made a significant global impact through Nollywood (the world's second-largest film industry by volume), Afrobeats music, contemporary Nigerian literature, fashion, and visual arts. The Mission works to showcase these creative industries and facilitate cultural exchange with Japan's own vibrant arts sector.

Cultural Programmes & Events

Nigerian Cultural Festival

Annual celebration of Nigerian arts, music, food, and heritage

The Mission organises an annual Nigerian Cultural Festival showcasing the diversity of Nigerian traditions through music, dance, costume, traditional cuisine, craft displays, and storytelling. The event is open to the public and draws hundreds of visitors from across Japan.

Art Exhibitions & Film Screenings

Nollywood screenings, contemporary visual art, photography

Throughout the year the Mission partners with local galleries, cinemas, and cultural institutions to host Nigerian art exhibitions and Nollywood film screenings. These events introduce Japan audiences to the depth and creativity of Nigerian artistic expression.

Literary & Academic Exchange

Author visits, lectures, and literary partnerships

Nigeria has produced some of the world's most celebrated authors, including Chinua Achebe, Wole Soyinka, Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie, and Ben Okri. The Mission facilitates literary exchanges, author visits, and academic dialogues that highlight Nigeria's contribution to world literature and scholarship.

Independence Day Celebrations

1 October — Nigeria's National Day reception

On October 1 each year, the Nigerian Embassy hosts an official National Day reception to mark Nigeria's independence anniversary. The event brings together members of the Nigerian community, Japan government officials, diplomats, and friends of Nigeria for an evening of celebration and goodwill.

Nigeria's Creative Industries

Nollywood

Nigeria's film industry produces over 2,500 films per year, making it the world's second-largest by volume. Nigerian films are now streamed globally on major platforms.

Afrobeats & Music

Nigerian music dominates global charts. Artists like Burna Boy, Wizkid, and Davido have introduced Afrobeats to audiences in Japan and worldwide.

Literature & Arts

Nigeria is home to Nobel Laureate Wole Soyinka and globally celebrated authors whose work has shaped 20th and 21st century world literature.

Fashion & Design

Nigerian fashion designers and Ankara fabric have gained recognition on international runways, with growing interest from Japan fashion houses.
