Economic Partnership Overview

Economic cooperation is a cornerstone of the Nigeria–Japan bilateral relationship. Both countries are committed to translating their political goodwill into tangible economic benefits for their citizens through structured frameworks of cooperation and joint institutional mechanisms.

Bilateral Trade Volume

Joint Commission

The Nigeria–Japan Joint Economic Commission meets annually to review progress, resolve barriers, and identify new sectors for enhanced cooperation.

Priority Sectors for Economic Cooperation

01

Energy & Natural Resources

Oil and gas upstream and downstream collaboration, renewable energy technology transfer, and power sector investment from Japan companies.

02

Agriculture & Food Security

Technology-driven agricultural transformation, including precision farming, food processing, and cold-chain infrastructure to reduce post-harvest losses.

03

Infrastructure & Construction

Japan firms are active participants in Nigeria's infrastructure development, including roads, railways, ports, and urban development projects.

04

Digital Economy & Innovation

Cooperation on digital infrastructure, fintech, e-governance, and support for Nigeria's growing technology and startup ecosystem.

05

Financial Services

Banking cooperation, insurance, and capital market linkages enabling greater flows of investment between the two economies.

For business matchmaking and investment facilitation enquiries, contact the Economic & Commercial Section: economic.tokyo@foreignaffairs.gov.ng
