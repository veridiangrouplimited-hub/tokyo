If you are a Nigerian in distress in Japan, the Embassy is here to help — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

How the Mission Can Help in an Emergency

The Nigerian Embassy in Japan provides consular assistance to Nigerians who find themselves in difficult or dangerous situations. Our consular officers can intervene, advocate on your behalf, and connect you with appropriate local services and legal resources.

Arrest or Detention

If you or a family member has been arrested

If a Nigerian is arrested in Japan, they have the right to request consular access. Japan authorities are required under the Vienna Convention on Consular Relations to notify the Nigerian Embassy without delay if a Nigerian national is arrested or detained. The Embassy can:

  • Visit you in custody and ensure your welfare
  • Provide a list of local lawyers (though cannot fund legal representation)
  • Liaise with Japan authorities on your behalf
  • Inform your family in Nigeria if you wish
  • Ensure you are not subject to torture or inhumane treatment

What to do: Immediately request that Japan authorities notify the Nigerian Embassy. If you cannot reach the Embassy, ask a trusted contact to call the emergency hotline.

Hospitalisation or Medical Emergency

Serious illness, accident, or medical crisis

If a Nigerian is seriously ill or hospitalised in Japan, the Embassy can assist by visiting, liaising with hospital authorities, and helping to contact next of kin in Nigeria. In cases of death abroad, the Embassy assists with the process of repatriating remains to Nigeria.

  • Hospital visits by consular staff
  • Contact with next of kin in Nigeria
  • Assistance with repatriation of remains
  • Referral to welfare support organisations

Lost or Stolen Documents

Passport, travel documents, or identity papers

If your Nigerian passport or other travel documents are lost or stolen in Japan, visit the Consular Section as soon as possible. You will need to file a report with Japan police first. The Embassy can issue an Emergency Travel Certificate to enable you to return to Nigeria pending a full passport replacement.

  • File a police report in Japan immediately
  • Visit or call the Consular Section with proof of Nigerian nationality
  • An Emergency Travel Certificate (ETC) can be issued within 24–48 hours in urgent cases

See also: Emergency Travel Certificate

Abuse, Trafficking, or Exploitation

Domestic violence, trafficking, or forced labour

The Embassy takes reports of human trafficking, domestic abuse, and labour exploitation seriously. Victims will be treated with confidentiality and referred to appropriate support services. If you are in immediate danger, call Japan emergency services first (Police: [number], Emergency: [number]), then contact the Embassy.

  • Confidential consultation with a consular officer
  • Referral to government and NGO support services in Japan
  • Safe return to Nigeria if required
The Embassy cannot: pay your legal fees, post bail, or intervene in the judicial process of Japan. However, we will always advocate for your rights and ensure you receive fair treatment.
